FORD MOTOR COMPANY
1926
In 1926, Henry Ford made a decision. At his Detroit factories, he cut his workers from six days down to five. Same pay. Forty hours instead of forty-eight. He wasn't being generous. He was running an experiment. He believed rested workers would produce more — and would have the time and money to spend during their leisure. Perhaps on his cars.

Two — Focus protection

FOUR CAVEATS
Because we are not here to sell you something.
  1. 100-80-100 means same output in less time. It is not less work. It is rearranged work. Companies that just chop a day off the calendar fail.
  2. Almost every pilot is self-selected. Companies that want this to work, opt in. Workers who want it to work, stay. Generalising is a stretch.
  3. The sectoral problem. Manufacturing shift work. Hospitals. Retail. Hospitality. You cannot cut a nurse's caseload by 20% without hiring more nurses — and nurses are already in shortage. Unsolved.
  4. Compressed ≠ reduced. Compressed hours can increase stress. Don't conflate the two models.
Now the caveats. Because we are not here to sell you something. One. The 100-80-100 model means the same output in less time. That is harder than it sounds. It is not "less work." It is rearranged work. Companies that don't redesign their workflows — that just chop a day off the calendar — fail. Two. Almost every pilot you've heard about tonight is self-selected. Companies that want this to work, opt in. Workers who want it to work, stay. Generalising those results to companies that don't want it is a stretch. Three. The sectoral problem. Manufacturing shift work. Hospitals. Frontline retail. Hospitality. You cannot cut a nurse's caseload by twenty percent without hiring more nurses — and nurses are already in shortage. The four-day week, in its reduced-hours form, was built in and for knowledge work. Scaling beyond is unsolved. Four — and this is the one I want you to remember — Belgium's law gives workers the right to compress forty hours into four longer days. That is not what Iceland and the UK studied. Compressed hours can actually increase stress. Don't conflate the two models.
